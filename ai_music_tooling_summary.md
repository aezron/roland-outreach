# AI And Music-Tooling Summary

This is a short summary of the AI-related and music-technology work I am most proud of and most relevant to Roland Future Design Lab style work.

## Litigma

Litigma is an AI-assisted workflow platform built around document-heavy workflows.

My work has included:

- building practical systems with Rust/Axum, React, Postgres, and async processing
- working on AI-assisted and automation-heavy workflows
- contributing across backend services, user-facing product behavior, and workflow design
- helping translate technically complex capabilities into software that is usable in practice

What I value most about this work is that it has required product thinking as well as engineering. The challenge has not only been getting systems to run, but making AI-assisted workflows understandable, reliable, and useful for real users.

## GANIC

GANIC is an AI-assisted drum sequencer project:
https://ganic.ronde.tools/

My contribution was focused on:

- graphic design
- product input
- testing
- user-perspective feedback

I was not responsible for model development, but the project gave me valuable exposure to how AI music ideas need to be shaped into creative tools that musicians can actually use.

## Music Technology And Live Sound

Alongside software work, I also bring a practical music-user perspective through live sound engineering and music-technology experience. I have worked in live music environments including Watergate and RSO, and I use Roland and BOSS products myself, including the SP-404MKII.

That matters to me because I think the best music tools are built not only from a technical point of view, but from a real understanding of how musicians work, experiment, and perform.
